My Story

The Search

A busy practice means constant interruptions. You forget to switch your timer from one matter to another. You forget to start your timer. You forget to stop your timer. By the end of the day, you're reconstructing hours of work from memory—defeating the entire purpose of time tracking. As a practicing lawyer, I spent years searching for a time tracking tool that actually understood the reality of knowledge work. Over these years I tried Chrometa, TimeCamp, Harvest, Clockify, and Toggl. Some tools tried to do too much. All of them required a ton of setup time. None of them fully delivered.

The History of Automated Time Tracking

Conceptually, automated time tracking should provide the solution—enabling more accurate billing to the right matter with less risk of human error. However, until recently, all automated time tracking systems required a ton of setup time. They were only accurate if you spent hours either carefully crafting and maintaining complex rule sets manually, or training early AI tools that constantly needed retraining whenever you acquired new clients. And even after all that setup, you still had to spend more time reviewing the automated records to make sure the system wasn't charging your time to the wrong client. The result? These systems would increase the amount of billable time captured and recorded, but often failed to deliver on the promise of saving you time.

The Breakthrough

With the recent emergence of large language models, the technology has finally reached the point where automated time tracking can deliver on that second promise and actually free up your time. AI can now understand context, learn patterns, and automate the tedious parts of time tracking while maintaining the accuracy and detail that legal billing and other knowledge work demands.

The Solution

SYNCOPaiD is the result of that journey. It's built by someone who understands the daily challenges of professional work because I live them. It's designed to capture every billable moment without interrupting your work. It learns your patterns, understands your matters, and creates accurate time entries automatically. This is the tool I needed—and now it exists.
